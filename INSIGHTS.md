# Strategic Insights — Workforce Pulse

**Source:** Workshop session (Challenge Statements Overview, U of Alabama)
**Track:** Workforce, Business & Economic Growth
**Region:** Montgomery, Alabama

---

## 1. Economic Structure of Montgomery

- **Largest employment category is government** — Montgomery is the seat of state government
- Heavy presence of municipal, state, and federal operations (Air Force)
- Dashboard must track **public sector employment trends**, not just private industry

## 2. Major Employers & Economic Anchors

### Manufacturing
- Hyundai plant (major employer) plus associated suppliers
- Manufacturing exists but is not the dominant statewide leader

### Defense & Federal Technology
- Maxwell Air Force Base and Gunter Air Force Base
- Technology hub of the U.S. Air Force — high-tech federal contracts
- Population turnover driven by military transitions

**Action items for our pipeline:**
- Track federal contracting signals
- Monitor defense tech job postings
- Capture workforce mobility trends

## 3. Big Tech Expansion

The Southeast U.S. (including Montgomery) is becoming a **data center hotspot**.

**Incoming / expanding companies:** AWS, Google, Meta, plus additional AI/data center projects pending.

**Key concerns raised:**
- Data centers require massive infrastructure but create limited direct jobs
- Impact on water, electricity, environment
- Questions about long-term economic value vs. local hiring vs. imported labor

**Dashboard opportunity — explicitly mentioned as a strategic concern:**
- Data center impact metrics
- Local hiring vs. imported labor ratios
- Infrastructure strain indicators
- Utility cost changes over time

## 4. Structural Economic Challenges

Montgomery has economically depressed neighborhoods, abandoned buildings, underutilized public land, and uneven growth (new growth zones vs. older decline zones).

**Workforce dashboard opportunity:**
- Overlay job growth vs. distressed areas
- Map employment access gaps
- Identify retraining needs by geography
- Detect workforce deserts

## 5. Workforce Characteristics

- Military-driven population turnover
- Government-heavy labor base
- Limited industrial diversity

**Dashboard should include:**
- Population mobility indicators
- Skill mismatch analysis
- Retention trends
- Career pipeline tracking

## 6. Public Safety (Cross-Track Opportunity)

- Police department is understaffed with heavy recruitment underway
- Public safety is a major political concern
- Interdisciplinary solutions were **explicitly encouraged**

**Workforce angle:** Recruitment analytics, police hiring funnel, staffing heatmaps, predictive modeling for officer shortages.

## 7. What Judges Want

### They value:
- Real-time data ingestion
- Actionable dashboards
- Something presentable to the **Mayor, Planning Department, Housing Development**
- Data-informed decision support
- **Customized specifically to Montgomery**

### They do NOT want:
- Generic job board clones
- Surface-level visualizations
- Purely theoretical ideas

### They want:
**Hard facts → Policy decisions → Strategic insight**

## 8. Montgomery Open Data Portal

- GIS-backed (likely Esri-based)
- Data can be downloaded, possibly API-accessible
- **Under-advertised and under-utilized**

**Our solution should:**
- Integrate city open datasets
- Improve visibility of open data
- Demonstrate API ingestion

## 9. Economic Development Priorities

Key areas mentioned: land use improvement, economic revitalization, business growth signals, workforce alignment, community engagement.

---

## Dashboard Feature Ideas (Aligned with Judge Expectations)

### A. Industry Demand Intelligence
- Real-time job scraping
- Sector heatmaps
- Public vs. private hiring ratios
- Federal contractor demand tracking

### B. Skills Gap Engine
- Compare job descriptions vs. local degree programs
- ASU program alignment insights
- Certification demand tracker

### C. Data Center Impact Monitor
- Utility usage modeling
- Hiring multiplier tracking
- Cost-of-living trend overlay
- Environmental risk signals

### D. Government Workforce Analytics
- Public sector hiring velocity
- Retirement pipeline projections
- Departmental vacancy heatmaps

### E. Economic Revitalization Map
- Job growth vs. abandoned property overlays
- Workforce access by neighborhood
- Investment targeting signals

### F. Recruitment Intelligence (Public Safety)
- Police hiring funnel dashboard
- Predictive staffing shortfall model
- Community response time vs. staffing ratio

---

## Strategic Differentiator

Judges explicitly said: *"Solutions must recognize the uniqueness of Montgomery."*

Include:
- Civil Rights heritage positioning
- Government-heavy economy modeling
- Air Force-driven workforce mobility
- Emerging tech hub transformation
- Southeastern infrastructure pressures

## High-Scoring Characteristics

- Real-time data ingestion
- Geospatial visualization
- Cross-track integration
- Policy-ready insights
- Clear decision-making value
- Customization to Montgomery
- Scalability to other cities

## Advanced Concept: Montgomery Workforce Digital Twin

A simulation dashboard that models economic impact of incoming data centers, forecasts workforce demand shifts, simulates infrastructure strain, suggests training investment allocations, and maps job growth vs. neighborhood revitalization.

Combines workforce + infrastructure + economic growth + smart city modeling. Interdisciplinary ideas were explicitly welcomed.

---

## Final Positioning

> **Frame Workforce Pulse as a decision-support system for the Mayor and Planning Department — not just a visualization app.**
>
> *"Montgomery's Strategic Workforce Intelligence Platform."*

This aligns with exactly what the judges and ASU stakeholders are seeking.
